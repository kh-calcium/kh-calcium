How do organisms utilize ion movement across a biological membrane to generate a specified response to stimuli?

Have a question? I might have an answer!

Having the opportunity to work in the Griffing Lab has given me the opportunity to gain experiences and skills I would have been missing otherwise, and I'm more thhan happy to share what I have learned!

What kind of experience do you have teaching?

I began teaching lab courses in the Fall of 2023. Since that time, I have taught Introductory Biology, Anatomy and Physiology, Biological Imaging, and Cell Biology courses.

How did you get into research?

I began researching as an undergraduate in the lab Dr. Lawrence Griffing at Texas A&M. From there, I have had the opportunity to expand my knowledge into a variety of different fields including cellular signaling and circadian processes.

What has been the most useful thing you have learned?

During my time working in labs, I have learned to enjoy whatever task in front of me in that moment. There will always be something else to do, but it is more important to focus on the task in front of you.

What would you say to someone interested in science?

Science is for everyone! As long as you have a desire ro learn and are willing to be taught, I believe that anyone can join the global community of science.

Where do you see yourself in the future?

I love working with microscopes and taking a look at new and interesting specimens. I also love the intensive analysis that comes with it, so I look forward to doing something similar in the future. I'm open to exploring the world, both physcially and in the scope of my career.

What are some of your hobbies?

For me, plants aren't just part of my job; I also love gardening in my free time! Besides that, I like to spend my time baking and working on different forms of art.
